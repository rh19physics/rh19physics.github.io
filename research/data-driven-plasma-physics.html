Research Thread Symbolic regression & scientific machine learning

My direct AI/ML research experience has so far focused on exploratory scientific machine-learning projects, especially interpretable model discovery. In 2024, I completed a symbolic-regression course project applying AI Feynman, the physics-inspired differentiable divide-and-conquer algorithm developed by Udrescu and Tegmark, to turbulent dissipation data generated from a gyrokinetic theoretical model. The goal was to infer an analytical expression for the ion-to-electron heating ratio \(Q_i / Q_e\) as a function of ion plasma beta \(\beta_i\) and the ion-to-electron temperature ratio \(T_i/T_e\). A key result was that the algorithm performed much better when supplied with a physically motivated dominant symbolic term, highlighting the importance of incorporating domain knowledge into scientific ML workflows.

This did not yield publishable results. But it got me interested in the concept of scientific machine learning (SciML), especially symbolic regression. I later did a tutorial talk on SciML at my department
